A City Planner's Introductory Guide for Sea Level Rise

Oceanography, Spring 2021

Introduction

The Problem: Sea Level Rise

Since 1900, climate-induced sea-level rise has heightend global waters approximately 8 inches, inducing increased storm intensities and and wide scale flooding in low-lying communities. Modern projections place another 12-inches of sea-level rise above the year 2000’s global sea averages. By 2100, we could see the oceans rise up to 2.5 meters. Such a rise would destroy low-altitude urban environments causing immesurable economic, cultural, and environmental damage.

Many communities have already borne witness to the devestating impacts of sea-level rise. Climatic events are only expected to increase in size and frequency, meaning we must begin preparing our communities for rising tides today. Hence, this City Planner's Introductory Guide to Sea Level Rise was developed to assist those developing our cities begin their research in how to prepare their communities.

The Interim Solution: Accomodation & Costal Defense

Accomodation and Costal Defenses refer to the necessary steps we must take to greenify / weatherize our communities and protect our coastlines, respectfully. We refer to these steps as interim solutions because they do not soley protect us from the far-reaching impacts of climate change and sea-level rise. Rather, they are practices we must incorporate into our larger struggle to stop the progress of climate change and reach a carbon nuetral society.

This guide is meant to serve as a city planner's introductory step for his or her research into sustainable infrastrucutre. Every community is unique and responsible for its own financial, cultural, and envrionmental security. As such, each city planner may use this introductory information (and attached readings - hyperlinked to the numbered circles below each section) to further his or her own reasearch in meeting the needs of their individual communities.
